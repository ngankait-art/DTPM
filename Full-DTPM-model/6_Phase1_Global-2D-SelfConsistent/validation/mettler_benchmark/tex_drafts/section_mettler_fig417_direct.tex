\section{Direct Benchmark Against Mettler Fig.~4.14 / 4.17}
\label{sec:mettler-fig417-direct}
%======================================================================

The 700\,W self-consistent reference case used in Figs.~\ref{fig:cross-section-F}--\ref{fig:radial-F-mettler} is the DTPM design operating point but is not the operating point at which Mettler's spatially resolved data were recorded.  His radial [F] dataset (Fig.~4.14 / 4.17 of~\cite{Mettler2025}) was taken at \SI{1000}{\watt} ICP power, \SI{10}{\milli\torr} total pressure, \SI{100}{sccm} total flow (varying SF$_6$/Ar ratio), and, for the wafer-radial panels, with \SI{200}{\watt} rf wafer bias applied.  To produce a like-for-like comparison we rerun the hybrid model at Mettler's actual power setting and at the two SF$_6$/Ar ratios he reports (90\,\% SF$_6$ and 30\,\% SF$_6$), with the wafer-bias term absent (L5 in \S\ref{sec:hybrid-limits}).

  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.98\textwidth]{fig_mettler_fig417_overlay}

All model curves are at \SI{1000}{\watt} rf, \SI{10}{\milli\torr}, with $\eta = 0.43$ (prescribed) and no wafer bias.  (a)~Normalised profile: both 90\,\% and 30\,\% SF$_6$ curves overlay Mettler's Fig.~4.14 cubic fit closely in the inner half ($r < 4$~cm) and converge to a 68\,\% drop at the edge, independent of composition.  (b)~Absolute [F] on a log scale: Mettler's bias-off points lie 2--4$\times$ above the model predictions.  (c)~Pointwise residuals $100(\text{model}-\text{Mettler})/\text{Mettler}$; both compositions fall outside the $\pm 10\,\%$ band (green) at every radius.}

\subsection{Shape Agreement and Composition Insensitivity}

Panel~(a) of Fig.~\ref{fig:mettler-fig417-direct} shows that the \emph{normalised} radial profile tracks Mettler's cubic fit within $\pm 10\,\%$ over the inner wafer ($r \leq 4$~cm) and falls slightly above the fit at the edge.  Critically, the model's 68\,\% centre-to-edge drop is nearly identical for both SF$_6$/Ar compositions, while Mettler's measured drop changes from 67\,\% (30\,\% SF$_6$) to 75\,\% (90\,\% SF$_6$).  This composition insensitivity is a direct signature of the fixed $\gamma_\mathrm{Al} = 0.18$ calibration: wall recombination sets the drop, and with $\gamma$ composition-independent the model has no mechanism to distinguish the two mixtures at fixed power.  The experimentally observed drop range is therefore an additional 8-percentage-point constraint on any future composition-dependent $\gamma$ parameterisation.

\subsection{Absolute Density Offset}

Panel~(b) shows that the model under-predicts the absolute wafer-centre [F] by a factor of $\sim 4$ for the 90\,\% SF$_6$ condition ($0.6\times10^{14}$~cm$^{-3}$ model vs.\ $2.5\times10^{14}$~cm$^{-3}$ Mettler) and by a factor of $\sim 1.7$ for the 30\,\% SF$_6$ condition ($0.35\times10^{14}$ vs.\ $0.60\times10^{14}$).  Two physical effects, both deliberately outside the Phase-1 scope, account for this:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Power coupling.}  The prescribed coupling efficiency $\eta = 0.43$ delivers only $P_\mathrm{abs} = $ \SI{430}{\watt} to the plasma for the nominal \SI{1000}{\watt} rf source.  Mettler's actual coupling efficiency at \SI{1000}{\watt} is not directly measured, but an ICP with a \SI{200}{\watt} rf wafer bias typically operates with $\eta \gtrsim 0.7$ (matching-network losses being the dominant sink at low match quality).  Under-coupling by a factor of $1.5$--$2.0$ translates directly to a proportional reduction in $n_e$, and hence in the F-atom source term (through the bilinear dissociation rate $k_\mathrm{diss}\,n_e\,\bar{n}_{\mathrm{SF}_6}$).
  \item \textbf{Wafer-bias-driven plasma expansion.}  Mettler's Fig.~4.17 explicitly compares bias-off and bias-on branches of the radial [F] profile; the bias-on curve is a factor of $\sim 1.6$ above the bias-off curve for 90\,\% SF$_6$, and up to $\sim 2.15$ at the wafer centre for 30\,\% SF$_6$~\cite{Mettler2025}.  The plasma volume expands into the process chamber when bias is applied, increasing the local electron-impact dissociation path length.  Because Mettler's probe measurements sampled intermittent bias-on / bias-off operation during the same reactor run, even his nominally ``bias-off'' data points may inherit a residual bias-on contribution from ion-driven wall sputtering and prior-cycle F atoms that have not yet fully recombined.
\end{enumerate}

The product of these two correction factors ($\sim 1.7\,\times\,1.6 \approx 2.7$ for 90\,\% SF$_6$) roughly recovers the observed $4\times$ offset; the residual $\approx 1.5\times$ gap at 90\,\% SF$_6$ is plausibly the combined signature of the missing electronegative ambipolar correction (L1) and any composition-dependent $\gamma$ residual.

\subsection{Takeaway}

Figure~\ref{fig:mettler-fig417-direct} is the most stringent benchmark in this report: it is simultaneously a \emph{shape} test (panel a), an \emph{absolute-magnitude} test (panel b), and a \emph{composition sensitivity} test (comparing the two curves in each panel).  The hybrid framework passes the shape test within $\pm 10\,\%$ over the inner wafer but fails both the absolute-magnitude and composition-sensitivity tests.  Neither failure is fatal: both decompose cleanly into the Phase-1 limitations already enumerated in \S\ref{sec:hybrid-limits} (specifically L1 on ambipolar transport and L5 on wafer-bias sheath physics).  Phase-2 addresses L5 directly with a capacitively coupled sheath extension that is projected to close the absolute-magnitude gap.
