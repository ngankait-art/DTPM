The present work's reference operating point is Mettler's canonical
spatial-benchmark condition: $P_\mathrm{ICP} = $ \SI{1000}{\watt},
$p = $ \SI{10}{\milli\torr}, \SI{100}{sccm} total flow (either 90\,\% or
30\,\% SF$_6$ with balance Ar), and \SI{200}{\watt} rf wafer bias (his
Fig.~4.14 and Fig.~4.17).  The self-consistent $\eta$ (§\ref{sec:selfconsistent-eta})
and the $m12$ capacitive-bias sheath (§\ref{sec:m12-bias-sheath}) let the
framework predict absolute [F] values at this operating point without
any post-hoc rescaling.

\subsection{Self-Consistent $\eta$}

Before the bias sheath even enters, the self-consistent ICP circuit
pipeline (§\ref{sec:selfconsistent-eta}) already substantially closes the
absolute-magnitude gap seen in the prior revision.  At \SI{1000}{\watt}
with $R_\mathrm{coil} = 0.8\,\Omega$, the converged coil current and
plasma loading give $\eta \approx 0.95$, delivering $P_\mathrm{abs}
\approx $ \SI{950}{\watt} --- more than double the old prescribed value
($0.43 \times 1000$\,W $=$ \SI{430}{\watt}) and consistent with Mettler's
operating regime where bias is applied.  The resulting
$\eta(P_\mathrm{rf})$ emergent curve across the full 200--1200\,W sweep
is shown in Fig.~\ref{fig:eta-sweep}.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.98\textwidth]{fig_eta_sweep}
  \caption{Emergent ICP circuit quantities across the power sweep at

  (a)~$\eta(P_\mathrm{rf})$ rises from $\sim 0.85$ at \SI{200}{\watt}
  to $\sim 0.96$ at \SI{1200}{\watt} as the plasma loading grows

\end{figure}

\subsection{Composition-Sensitivity Blind Test}

Figure~\ref{fig:composition-blind-test} presents the most stringent
validation of the $m12$ bias-sheath calibration.  The dimensionless
parameter $\lambda_\mathrm{exp} = 3.20$ was tuned once, against
Mettler's 90\% SF$_6$ bias-on centre [F] enhancement ($\times 1.60$),
and then applied unchanged to the 30\% SF$_6$ case as a blind test.

\begin{figure}[H]
  \centering
  \includegraphics[width=0.80\textwidth]{fig_composition_blind_test}
  \caption{Wafer-bias [F] enhancement (bias-on / bias-off ratio at the
  wafer centre) at \SI{1000}{\watt}, \SI{10}{\milli\torr},
  \SI{200}{\watt} bias.  The model's $\lambda_\mathrm{exp} = 3.20$ is
  calibrated on the 90\% SF$_6$ branch only (deviation $+0.8\,\%$); the
  same calibration yields a $-12.8\,\%$ deviation on the blind-test
  30\% SF$_6$ branch, within the $\pm 20\,\%$ acceptance band.  The
  under-prediction at 30\% SF$_6$ reflects a stronger plasma-volume
  expansion in the more electropositive Ar-rich regime, where the
  reduced-order expansion model over-averages across compositions.}
  \label{fig:composition-blind-test}
\end{figure}

\subsection{What Has Been Resolved vs What Remains}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Resolved (L6 of prior revision)}: Power-coupling efficiency
  is now self-consistent.  The Lieberman coupled-circuit model gives
  $\eta$ as a physical observable, not a prescribed dial.
  \item \textbf{Resolved (L5 of prior revision)}: Wafer-bias sheath is
  now modelled.  The $m12$ module captures the ion-heating-induced
  plasma-volume expansion with a single calibration parameter and passes
  the composition blind test.
  \item \textbf{Remaining (L1, unchanged)}: Electronegative ambipolar
  correction is still omitted.  Responsible for the residual outer-wafer
  over-prediction in Fig.~\ref{fig:mettler-fig417-v2}(b)--(c).
  \item \textbf{Remaining (L2-L4, unchanged)}: Electron-energy conduction,
  steady-state assumption (no E-H transition), single wall temperature.
\end{itemize}
